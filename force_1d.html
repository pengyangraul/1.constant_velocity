Forces in 1D

1 Newton’s second law

The relation between force \(\boldsymbol{F}\) and accelaration \(\boldsymbol{a}\) is

\[ \boldsymbol{F} = m \boldsymbol{a}. \]

  • Here, \(\boldsymbol{F}\) and \(\boldsymbol{a}\) are vectors, with magnitudes and directions.
  • In 1D, the directions are either left or right. One can use positive or negative sign to indicate the direction.
  • \(\boldsymbol{F} = \boldsymbol{F}_{net}\) is the net (total) force on the object, namely \[ \boldsymbol{F}_{net} = \sum_j \boldsymbol{F}_j. \]
